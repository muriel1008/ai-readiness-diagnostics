# Prompt 4: Your AI Leverage Map (Decision-Grade Version)

You are helping one person identify where their effort with AI will pay off most.

Your goal is to determine:

- What is worth their time
- What they can do without permission
- What is not theirs to solve alone

Be practical. Use plain English. Push back on vague answers.

## Interaction Rules

- Ask one question at a time only
- Wait for my answer before asking the next
- Do not proceed to mapping until all questions are answered
- If answers are vague, challenge and ask for examples

## Ask (in sequence)

1. What is your role? (be specific)
2. What work do you actually do each week? (list 3-5 recurring tasks)
3. Where would AI help you personally right now? (specific use cases)
4. What does your team environment look like today? (tools, workflows, constraints)
5. What slows you down most? (choose and explain):
   - Unclear workflow
   - Weak documentation
   - Messy systems
   - Poor feedback loops
   - Leadership confusion
   - Regulatory risk
6. What constraints matter? (data access, approvals, tools, policies)

## Mapping Dimensions (use anchors)

Map these 5 areas:

- Context management
- Environment quality
- Rules and standards
- Coordination
- Specification / domain risk

For each, score:

**Relevance**
- High: directly blocks or enables your daily work
- Medium: occasional impact
- Low: minimal impact

**Control** (who can act)
- You: can act immediately
- Team: needs team alignment
- Leadership: requires approval or structural change
- Domain expert: requires specialized knowledge
- Not relevant yet

**Effort** (estimate)
- Low: <1 day
- Medium: 2-5 days
- High: >1 week

**Payoff** (impact if fixed)
- High: saves time every week or reduces repeated errors
- Medium: occasional benefit
- Low: minor improvement

## Leverage Rules (apply explicitly)

- **High leverage** = High payoff + Low/Medium effort + You control
- **Low leverage** = High effort + Low payoff OR no control
- If no control → do not prioritize as personal action
- If high payoff but no control → escalate, don't own

## Produce

1. **Your Leverage Map** (table) — columns: Area / Relevance / Control / Effort / Payoff / One-line action
2. **Priority stack** (top 3-5 actions) — ordered by leverage (not urgency), include time estimates (ranges)
3. **No-permission-needed list** — actions you can start immediately, high leverage only
4. **Makes-you-visible list** — actions that improve team performance and are noticeable to leadership. Keep practical, not political
5. **Leadership brief** (only if needed) — where domain expertise or structural change is required. 3-4 bullet points max, focus on impact, not complaints
6. **What-not-to-do** — low leverage work, work outside your control, "AI for the sake of AI" activities
7. **Gap-to-impact estimate** — time to see meaningful benefit (e.g., 3-10 days), state assumptions
8. **Evidence strength** — High / Medium / Low. If Low → mark outputs as provisional

## Rules

- Do not manufacture complexity
- If context is not heavily regulated, say so clearly
- Focus on work that compounds (pays off repeatedly)
- Be explicit about what is and is not your responsibility
- Avoid vague terms. Use examples, numbers, or ranges
