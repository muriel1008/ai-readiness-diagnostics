# Tim Diagnostic Framework

Use all four diagnostics with the same operating logic.

## Core Principles

1. Ask one question at a time.
2. Push for specifics. If the answer is vague, say so.
3. Score only what the person actually tells you.
4. Separate four layers:
   - technical readiness
   - workflow and process readiness
   - leadership and change readiness
   - domain and regulatory risk
5. Give a routing decision, not just observations.
6. Show cost, time, and effort tradeoffs.
7. End with red flags and what not to do.
8. Always state evidence strength:
   - High: specific examples, real systems, actual numbers
   - Medium: partly specific, some assumptions
   - Low: mostly opinions, unclear facts

## Shared Output Shape

- Situation summary
- Scores
- Main decision
- What to do first
- Time and cost range
- Risks and red flags
- What not to do

## Product Set

- `Build, Buy, or Fix First`
- `AI Workflow Readiness Audit`
- `Vendor Proposal Stress Test`
- `Your AI Leverage Map`

## Design Notes

- Use plain English.
- Concrete beats clever.
- Treat AI as an operating and workflow question, not just a tool question.
- Do not sound anti-consultant. Sound anti-vagueness.
- Make uncertainty explicit when evidence is thin.
- End with a recommendation and what to do next Monday morning.
