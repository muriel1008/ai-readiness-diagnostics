# AI Readiness Diagnostics

Most teams do not have an AI problem first. They have a workflow, environment, and decision-quality problem.

I help leaders, team leads, and internal AI champions answer four practical questions:

- Should we build this ourselves, buy help, or fix the basics first?
- Is our workflow and environment actually ready for AI to work well?
- Is this vendor proposal worth paying for, or are we being sold expensive commodity work?
- Where should one person or one team focus first so effort compounds instead of scattering?

## What I Offer

### 1. Build, Buy, or Fix First

A decision diagnostic for leaders before they commit budget, sign a consulting engagement, or launch an AI initiative.

You get:

- a scored view of technical readiness, workflow readiness, leadership readiness, and risk
- a routing decision
- cost and time comparison between options
- specific first actions
- questions to ask any vendor or consultant

### 2. AI Workflow Readiness Audit

A practical audit of how work actually gets done today and what needs to be cleaned up before AI will help instead of create noise.

You get:

- a readiness scorecard
- a prioritized fix list
- a draft operating guide structure
- workflow rules that reduce ambiguity
- a realistic estimate of how many days of work separate you from "ready enough"

### 3. Vendor Proposal Stress Test

A line-by-line review of an AI consulting proposal, pitch, or statement of work.

You get:

- a breakdown of what is real expertise versus standard implementation work
- likely cost delta between doing parts internally and buying the full proposal
- pushback questions
- a cleaner counter-proposal structure

### 4. Your AI Leverage Map

A personal diagnostic for the person trying to make AI work inside the organization.

You get:

- a map of what is yours to solve and what is not
- a priority stack of actions
- a no-permission-needed list
- deliverables worth sharing upward
- a short brief for leadership if domain or regulatory risk is real

## How I Work

- I ask for specifics, not slogans.
- I score only what is actually supported by evidence.
- I separate technical issues from workflow issues, leadership issues, and domain-risk issues.
- I do not default to "buy consultants."
- I do not default to "build everything yourself."
- I aim for a clear decision, a short action list, and fewer wasted months.

## Good Fit

- You are deciding where to invest next.
- You want AI to support real work, not just demos.
- You need a sharper view before spending money.
- You want practical next steps, not a slide deck.

## Not A Fit

- You want a generic AI keynote.
- You want hype, not diagnosis.
- You want a vendor to tell you what you already decided to buy.

## Typical Outcomes

- clearer decision-making
- smaller, better-scoped pilots
- fewer expensive distractions
- better internal alignment
- more confidence about what to fix first

If useful, this can lead into a workshop, advisory sprint, or follow-up implementation support. The diagnostic comes first because the decision quality matters more than the tool choice.
